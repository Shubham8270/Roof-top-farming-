Household Carbon Credit Generation through Rooftop Farming

Households can generate carbon credits by adopting rooftop farming practices. Rooftop farming helps in reducing the carbon footprint by absorbing CO2, reducing the urban heat island effect, and promoting local food production.

For example, a household that converts its rooftop into a garden can significantly reduce its carbon footprint and generate carbon credits annually.

Earnings from Carbon Credits

The earnings from carbon credits can vary based on the market price and the amount of carbon reduction achieved. On average, households can earn between $100 to $500 per year from selling their carbon credits.

The earnings can be higher for households that implement more extensive carbon reduction measures through rooftop farming.
